Essay 1: UX & UI Analysis

While interaction on the web is structured through the user interface (UI) and (UX), the ethics of the interaction design can be deliberated since the way users interact with websites is largely shaped by the principles of UI/UX design. These elements not only determine how information is presented and navigated but also influence the accessibility, inclusivity, and ethical considerations embedded within digital platforms. This essay attempts to examine the Dischem website, a well-known South African pharmacy, as a case study to analyse how its UI and UX structures web interactions. The analysis will explore the effectiveness of its design choices, the inclusivity of its user experience, and the ethical implications of its interaction design by particularly focusing on accessibility, transparency, and digital inclusion. By examining the Dischem website within the context of ethical interaction design, this essay aims to highlight both the strengths and areas for improvement when creating design experiences that serve all users. This will primarily be achieved by using the following frameworks as a source: “Ethical UX Design and User Trust: Why It Matters?” by Aparna K. S, and lastly, “Ethical Design: Why Is It Critical for UX Designers?” by Juned Ghanchi.

Aparna K.S. describes UX in design ethics as the moral guidelines that shape our design decisions, then goes on to further explain that these principles serve the users' best interests instead of taking advantage of them, whether deliberately or unintentionally (Aparna K.S., 2024). This takes us to the question: What is UI? UI is a point where humans interact and communicate with a computer or device. It encompasses elements such as display screens, keyboards, a mouse, etc. However, this can also refer to how users may engage with applications or, in this case, websites, through visual and auditory components ranging from visual design or input controls to sound effects. Thus, an effective UI follows key design principles that help users navigate smoothly and accomplish their goals with ease. This goes hand-in-hand with UX. (Hashemi-Pour and Churchville, 2024)

UX describes the emotions and understanding that users have when interacting with a product, application, service, or, in this case, website. While it is a broad concept that encompasses factors such as ease of navigation and usability, it also represents the complete journey a user goes through while using a particular product or website service (ProductPlan, 2024). Therefore, it not only involves their direct interactions but also considers how the website service supports their broader goals and tasks (ProductPlan, 2024). Together, they become essential when creating effective, engaging, and user-friendly design experiences for all users.

Therefore, by analysing the UI and UX of the Dischem website, it can be shown how the site structures interaction and addresses ethical considerations in its design. Dischem is the second-largest pharmacy chain in South Africa, and it demonstrates a utilitarian approach to UI/UX design with room for improvement.

The UI design for Dischem consists of information organisation, category-based navigation, responsive design and compositional layouts. The visual design layout for their product organisation is extensive, providing an easy navigation for users and their specific needs. It is worth mentioning that product organisation ranges from category to being able to filter according to price and discounts, which adds functionality to the website design. This method of organisation simplifies the browsing experience for the user, allowing them to intuitively navigate items quickly. In addition, the sections have category-based navigation, such as "Easter Delights”, “Shop for Mother’s Day” or “Guard Against the Flu” (accounting for the change in seasons), provide thematic organisation and allow users to navigate according to seasons, keeping the user’s needs in mind. This adds to the ethical design usability, which refers to how efficiently and satisfactorily a user can accomplish a specific goal (Ghanchi, 2021). That being said, there is room for improvement in the UI of Dischem’s website, such as the responsive design. The responsive design for the navigation bar, search bar, and burger-button drop-down is inconsistent across different devices. The alignment of content is also not consistent, from products to banners. During the Easter Season, the homepage of the website showcased easter specials. However, these banners/images had different sizes, resulting in blank white space and visual disharmony. With regards to products on the homepage, the compositional layout was also inconsistent. Block cards for products would be of the same size however, the product's image displayed would be of different sizes, not to mention that the text on each block card started on different spaces on the block cards, resulting in visual disharmony again. The product button on the block card would also be in different spaces, thus producing disorientated users due to a frustrating layout. Concerning banners, some would cut out the content text on the page, or layer it onto a product/image, adding to a challenging UI to read and a UX of slight disorientation.

The UX design of the Dischem website prioritises transactional efficiency over engagement. This echoes their pharmacy-first focus, whereby prescription management and health product searches are streamlined, aligning with their core mission (Simplify.hr, 2016). In addition, their post-purchase tracking integration with AfterShip allows order tracking via confirmation emails, reducing customer service inquiries (Team, 2020). They do create convenience for their users since they incorporate in-store perks as well, such as linking loyalty cards, personalised recommendations, and notably, the ability to order medical prescriptions directly through the app for collection at a selected store. This integrates pharmacy services, making healthcare more accessible and convenient for users. Despite these strengths, the UX reveals challenges in real-time communication and service consistency, with users reporting delays and insufficient updates on order statuses. Accessibility features appear limited, this could potentially exclude users with disabilities.

In addition, on the order tracking website, users are able to link back to the Dischem website, view their returns policy, and view the tracking page. Some points of friction to note are the cookie consent, whereby the banner’s minimal design may inadequately inform users about data collection practices, leaning into design ethical considerations as well.

The ethical considerations of Dischem meet basic compliance but include granular room for improvement. The cookies policy complies with basic regulations but does not offer users detailed controls over their data preferences. This means that health-related purchases, such as medication, lack strict data privacy, which is not explicitly highlighted. While Dischem is a business, another ethical consideration is its price integrity. This stems from the criticism faced due to inflating mask prices during COVID-19 (Competition Tribunal, 2020). With regards to accessibility, the Dischem website has limited evidence of screen-reader compatibility or alt-text for product images, excluding visually impaired users, but includes phone support. In addition, while Dischem’s interface is primarily a white background, they do not offer a dark mode, excluding users' “visual comfort levels” and lighting conditions (Ghanchi, 2021).

Overall, Dischem’s UI/UX design demonstrates thoughtful integration of digital health services but requires ongoing refinement in communication transparency, accessibility, and user support to fully meet customer expectations and ethical standards. This concludes that their UI/UX and ethical design meet compliance but have room for improvement. Lastly, while their website design effectively serves its commercial goals, there is a need for intentional shifts to prioritise user experience and inclusivity. By addressing these gaps, the platform could reinforce its position as a trusted healthcare partner in South Africa.
