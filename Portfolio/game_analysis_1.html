Coffee Talk Analysis

Coffee Talk (2020)> is a visual novel by Toge Productions (in Unity Engine), whereby the player is a barista to a coffee shop that only opens at night. The player brews coffee and listens to customers(mix of human and non-human, such as elves and succubi) unveil stories with a warm cup or two. The game has story-driven gameplay by focusing on character interactions and storytelling, with players learning about the lives and struggles of fantastical beings in an alternative Seattle. Instead of the branching narratives being traditional dialogue choices, the branching paths are influenced by how players serve drinks, adding a unique twist to the genre. This allows players to influence the story through drink preparation, which serves as a form of interaction rather than direct dialogue choices. As Jose P. Zagal (2010) mentions, to better understand a game it is important to understanding its components, how they interact, and how they facilitate certain experiences in players (Zagal, 2010, p.33). This essay explores how Coffee Talk innovates the visual novel genre by using drink preparation as the main decision-making mechanic, replacing traditional dialogue choices. This will be done by using Zagal’s Ludoliteracy lens (Zagal, 2010).

The brewing mechanic is one of the core mechanics of the game. To brew, players use certain ingredients for certain drinks, and players can serve drinks containing 4 statuses: warm/cool/sweet/bitter (Toge Productions, 2019). This affects the dialogue that follows, since the customers comment on the drink served. (e.g. the werewolf Gala, who requires a werewolf remedy to calm his fury close to the full moon, and only tells you keywords, and it is up to the player to guess the correct ingredients.) The brewing becomes light puzzle-solving through interpretive reasoning by interpreting what the player wants (if the player says I want something that is “sweet and warm”).

A moment of frivolous play that adds to the coffee shop immersion is when the players can add their latte art onto a drink before serving (provided the drink has milk as an ingredient). Interesting to mention, in the latte art mechanic, there is a fluid simulation which is the consistency and the flow of milk when it is poured to create a latte art (Lasheli Dwitri Witjaksana, 2019). This means that the outcome of the art is dependent on the player and not just some automated art. This is an optional creative feature that adds play to the atmosphere.

These mechanics, combined with the dialogue, are what build relationships and branch the story further. This can be tracked by the phone system, which basically tracks people's posts and is a great way of keeping track of who you've met in the game and who you have yet to meet. In accord with Zagal, In addition to being able to pick out elements of a game’s design, it is important to understand how the interaction between these elements helps create a certain experience for the player (Zagal, 2010, p.33).

Now the game balances the mechanics by revolving the mechanics around its barista simulation elements, such as allowing the player to do the drink preparation and latte art, but having a no-fail drinks system. This system ensures every drink is valid, though some may not meet customer expectations. This avoids frustration while still encouraging experimentation (Toge Productions, 2019). The balance lies in how the mechanics enhance the storytelling, such as the act of brewing coffee. It is not just a gameplay feature, but a narrative tool that shapes character interactions and story outcomes. While the mechanics are relatively simple, they are deeply tied to the dynamics of listening, empathy, and relationship-building. This ensures that neither aspect overshadows the other but instead works in harmony to deliver a unique experience. By blending these elements seamlessly, Coffee Talk creates an experience where gameplay mechanics serve as a vehicle for storytelling, making it more than just a visual novel or a simulation game.

Together, this creates an interesting dynamic that focuses on storytelling and character interaction by adding narrative depth, atmosphere again and character relationship. The narrative depth includes the game’s usage of the visual novel format, where players listen to customers' stories and help them through meaningful conversations. The absence of direct dialogue choices shifts the focus to how drinks influence character moods and story progression. (Gamedeveloper.com, 2018) Secondly, the atmosphere is a cozy coffee shop setting, lo-fi music, and pixel art visuals that create a relaxing environment, complementing the narrative-driven gameplay (Gamedeveloper.com, 2018). Lastly, the character relationships within the game are moulded by interactions with fantastical characters, allowing players to build relationships and explore societal themes like social class and personal struggles in a subtle manner. (Like the sucubis and the elves’ relationship, whereby the elf’s family doesn’t approve of the succubus).

This ties into how the theme is executed and how they contribute to the overall experience. It is not just apparent through narrative, but also illustrated through aesthetics, mechanics, and system (Lasheli Dwitri Witjaksana, 2019). This means that the impact of 90s pixel art with anime-inspired graphics art direction and the Lofi chill-hop tunes reinforces that experience of a coffee shop that is also a safe place for stories to be heard. It is also hard to miss the societal themes that are subtly conveyed through fantastical narratives, engaging players in thoughtful reflection. I particularly enjoyed how the main customer is a writer who is writing a book about a world with only humans. The other characters find that idea crazy, but she then mentions that while humans will be the only race, there is still a way that certain topics still arise (such as race, class, etc). And it is an interesting dynamic to see because those topics are still present within that fictional world of different races (humans, elves) and classes.

This creates a unique visual novel experience. As reported by Zagal, to understand a game, it is often important to understand its context with relation to other games as well as gaming conventions and mechanics that might be common across multiple genres (Zagal, 2010, p.31). Visual novels are characterised by narrative-driven gameplay, often with branching storylines and player choices. Coffee Talk follows these conventions through story-driven gameplay and branching narratives while also following the sub-genre of interactive fiction and simulation by emphasising storytelling combined with player interaction. However, it diverges in the sense that, unlike typical visual novels, Coffee Talk avoids dialogue trees and instead uses drink-making as the primary decision-making mechanic. (Giant Bomb, 2022)

Coffee Talk innovates the visual novel genre by using drink preparation as the main decision-making mechanic, replacing traditional dialogue choices. This approach deepens player engagement, fosters empathy, and supports storytelling, while its cozy atmosphere and subtle social themes create a uniquely immersive and reflective experience.
